Dodge Durango has been two kinds of SUVs since it came out in 1998 -- a truck-based, body-on-frame type and a unibody type. In other words, it's been both an SUV and a crossover. It's even been both a mid-size and a full-size one. Notable model years include 2001, when the model got an interior redesign with upgrades and some exterior updates, 2002, when a new and exciting SXT package became available, and 2007, when it got a facelift.

Trims have ranged widely over the years. They've included base SLT, ST (also known as SE), Sport, and the luxurious SLT Plus and Limited. There have also been off-road-themed Adventurer and powerful R/T models. When the third generation came out in 2011, more trims came with it. They ranged from Express, Crew, and Crew Plus to GT, premium Citadel, and high-performance SRT Hellcat. There were also redesigns in 2014, some new packages in 2015, and a few more upgrades in 2016.
