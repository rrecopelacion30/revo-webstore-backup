About the Mitsubishi Mirage

As duos of sedans and hatchbacks popular for their practicality go, Mirage cars -- they're it. They're dependable drivers for the widely traveled, for one, with a RepairPal Reliability Rating of 4.5 out of 5.0, ranking it 7th out of 21 subcompact cars evaluated. Secondly, they're loaded with a lot of the tech one on the go would need, from auto climate control to touchscreen infotainment, and what's more, they're safe bets, with great ratings from the IIHS and NHTSA and features like forward collision warning and automatic braking to help back it up. They're just as fuel-efficient, too -- these days, you can look forward to gas mileage in the 30 mpg range for city driving and in the 40s for cruising the highway. Best of all, they're affordable, low-cost to fix up and even cheaper to customize. Later models even support that with a super 10-year/100,000-mile powertrain warranty, some with complimentary maintenance like oil filter changes, engine and cabin air filter replacement, and tire rotations for the first two years or 30,000 miles of ownership.

FAQs About Mitsubishi Mirage Parts and Accessories

Can I customize my Mirage?

The car's cost-effective to own, for one; Mitsubishi retails it for $16,695 to start with, while MotorTrend, putting it at #2 on its list of Cheapest New Cars for 2024, prices it at $18,110 or so. Car and Driver, in a recent review, puts the base S trim at around $18,000, the LE trim at $18,700 or so, and Black Edition, Ralliart, and SE at $19,300 plus and a little more than $20,000, respectively. It's also cheap to maintain; RepairPal cites low annual repair costs, less of a likelihood to end up at a Mitsubishi service center for repairs, and a low chance that repairs are serious. In short, its prospects are near-endless.

Can I mod the engine in my Mirage?

Yes. Consider the 2024 model. Add a turbocharger, a cold air intake, and a performance engine air filter to its 1.2-liter, three-cylinder engine, and you can get a few extra horses and pound-feet of torque -- the result being a more exciting ride. Customize the exhaust and muffler, and you'll get higher efficiency and an attention-getting rev, too.

What's the best way to use the cargo space in my Mirage?

If it's a 2024 model, it's got plenty -- 47 cubic feet, max -- so you'll want to add accessories like a cargo tray and net to coordinate everything, plus a roof rack and crossbars to make even more room (for outdoor gear like bikes and kayaks, for instance).

My Mirage is an investment. How can I protect its value?

By installing accessories like all-weather floor mats, custom seat covers, and sunshades. They'll stave off damage from dirt, spills, and solar heat and light, helping you save plenty of money in the long run.

Why should I buy OEM parts and accessories for my Mirage and not aftermarket?

They'll be Mitsubishi-quality, meaning they'll fit right and work well the first time. Most importantly, they'll look the part. They'll even be OEM-warrantied, too, in most cases. That's peace of mind you may not get on the aftermarket.
