A Brief History of the Mitsubishi Outlander Sport

Outlander Sport may seem another small Mitsubishi SUV in a crowded subcompact segment; it's anything but. The crossover's got a solid reputation for mannerly driving, for safety, for being a great value equipment-wise, and above all, for dependability. Heavyweights in the auto industry -- experts from Edmunds and MotorTrend to Car and Driver and Kelley Blue Book's KBB.com -- all vary on this theme, pointing out that the crossover is a good city car, decently equipped for the low price (the latest models, with standard all-wheel drive, no less); that it has the tech basics you need; that it's got fair fuel-efficiency coupled with good looks; and that it's got a super warranty. Above all, they say, it's an affordable one. Even RepairPal points out how reliable it is, giving it a 4.0 out of 5.0 Reliability Ranking -- a 7th-place spot among 26 compact SUVs, by recent data.

FAQs About Mitsubishi Outlander Sport Parts and Accessories

Can I upgrade my Outlander Sport?

The SUV's inexpensive to own and maintain, so customizing is something you can do even more cheaply. Its MSRP from Mitsubishi, for 2024, for instance, is only around $24,000, and Car and Driver magazine market-prices the ES, LE, SE trims between a little over $27,000 and just under $30,000. The custom-like Trail Edition and Ralliart trims and the SEL trim at the top of the line weigh in at around and a little more than $30,000, respectively. RepairPal points out the lower-than-average ownership costs, plus less of a likelihood to show up at a Mitsubishi dealer for out-of-the-ordinary service, and a low chance that repairs will be bad enough to end up running up a bill.

Can my Outlander Sport engine get more horsepower and torque?

Say you drive the 2024 model, with either the standard 148-horsepower, 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine or the available 168-horsepower, 2.4-liter. To get a few more horses and pound-feet, you'll have to enrich its air-to-fuel ratio by adding a turbocharger, a cold air intake, and a performance engine air filter.

How can I use the cargo space in my Outlander Sport best?

The 2024 model, for instance, with 21.7 to 49.5 cubic feet of trunk space, can benefit from accessories like a cargo tray and net to coordinate luggage and, to expand capacity, a roof rack and crossbars. The latter is great for transporting outdoor equipment like bikes and kayaks, too.

How can I maintain the value of my Outlander Sport?

By protecting your investment: put in all-weather floor mats to repel dirt and muck, custom seat covers to help prevent seat damage from spills, and sunshades to help keep pricey electronics from frying. They'll help you save money in the long run, too.
