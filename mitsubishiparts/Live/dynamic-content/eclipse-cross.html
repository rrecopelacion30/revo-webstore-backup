A Brief History of the Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross

Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross is a compact crossover SUV that debuted to acclaim in 2018. It's been popular ever since, and not only for the sporty all-wheel-drive (AWD) handling that makes it a blast to drive. For its quiet, comfortable ride, too, for one, not to mention its well-equipped interior, ample cargo space, and great looks. What's more, with a stellar warranty, it's sure to remain sought-after by commuters, families, and highway pleasure-cruisers everywhere.

FAQs About Eclipse Cross Parts and Accessories

How much does Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross cost?

A 2024 model can go for just under $27,000 MSRP. A used model, however -- say, a 2020 Eclipse Cross -- might run just under $18,000 to start, according to recent data from Kelley Blue Book's KBB.com. On Edmunds, similarly, models are running for just under $19,000 to start. At any rate, this pricing means plenty of money saved for upgrades -- a bike carrier, mud guards, scuff plates, you name it.

How reliable is Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross?

Fairly. Reviewers on Kelley Blue Book, for example, rate the 2024 model a respectable 3.3 out of 5. Reviewers of the 2024 model on Edmunds, 3.9. Moreover, US News & World Report rates the SUV 79 out of 100, according to a J.D. Power prediction. You can keep yours just as dependable; just be sure to stay on top of regular service -- stick to the schedule in your owner's manual -- and take care of problems common to the line sooner rather than later.

What is my Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross maintenance schedule?

That depends on what your manual says. Generally, however, you should inspect and, if needed, replace the brake pads and brake rotors, service the suspension, and change the air filter every 7,500 miles, fill transmission fluid and inspect brake hoses and driveshaft boots every 15,000 miles, service the fuel hoses, drive belts, and exhaust every 30,000 miles, and so on.

What are some common Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross problems?

According to CoPilot, you might run into a faulty clutch, a defective brake pedal, or bad door handles occasionally. For these and other issues, if you're not sure what to do, you're welcome to get in touch with a Mitsubishi dealer.
