About the Mitsubishi Lancer

Originally a standout coupe of the sports car-smitten 1970s, Lancer is today an iconic Mitsubishi sports car -- with a history that lives on long after having gone out of production in 2017. What were successive iterations in liftback and hatchback body styles elsewhere through the 1980s and 1990s became, in the US, in 2001, the sedan that would write the myth that would become that legend. In popular trims ranging from GT and Ralliart to Sportback LS and Sportback Ralliart, Lancer would be known for more than a touch of sport. It'd be the powerful, lightweight performance enthusiast's car, with the great stopping power and exceptional cornering writ even larger by its high-performance, blast-to-drive sibling, the Lancer Evolution, a popular rally car from its inception globally in 1992 and in the US in 2003. Most of all though, Lancer would become known not only for its dependability, but also its affordability -- as the perfect car for custom mods.

FAQs About Mitsubishi Lancer Parts and Accessories

Can I upgrade my Lancer?

Since it's cost-effective to both own and maintain -- the market value of a Lancer can run anywhere from $8,000 to $18,000, according to most auto review and pricing authorities -- it's cheaper to customize, and so, it's got plenty of prospects.

Can I mod my Lancer engine?

Yes. To get more horsepower and torque, add a turbocharger, swap out the factory air intake for a cold air intake, and mod the exhaust to a cat-back system with wider tubing and better muffler; you'll get the better airflow you need for it. Also, upgrade to a high-flow catalytic converter to increase exhaust flow from the engine.

Can I customize the look of my Lancer?

You can go several ways here. For one, deck it out with a body kit, and add accent trim. Secondly, put on a spoiler or wing, or even a hood scoop. Third, install aluminum alloy wheels, and top things off with custom wheel accessories like locks and center caps.

How can I protect the value of my Lancer?

Keeping its interior in top shape is key. For starters, update your factory floor mats and seats; swap out the mats for all-weather floor mats and add custom seat covers. They'll help safeguard the plastics and upholstery from dirt and spills. Then, put in sunshades; they'll reduce the damaging effects of light and heat on the electronics. Also, store the car out of the elements when you aren't driving it -- namely, under a car cover.
