About the Mitsubishi Montero

A full-size SUV of the 1980s, 1990s and early 2000s, preceding the wildly popular Mitsubishi Outlander and its Mitsubishi Endeavor sibling, Montero remains a ride of choice among off-roaders and families alike. Why? In trims ranging from base, Sport, LS, and RS to LX, SR, Limited, ES, and XLS, it's reliable yet ruggedly trail-ready, with capable powertrains, plenty of space for passengers and gear, and the comfort-level to help you get there in style.

FAQs About Montero Parts and Accessories

Why was Montero discontinued?

Because if the growing popularity of crossovers like Outlander and Endeavor, resulting in Mitsubishi shifting its focus. Of course, that means can get one cheap enough used these days that it's a great prospect for mods and upgrades.

What was the last year Mitsubishi made the Montero?

2006. According to Kelley Blue Book's KBB.com, one of those can go for $6,000 or so used. That means plenty of cash left over for performance upgrades like a supercharger, exterior improvements like tougher steel wheels and bumpers, cargo accessories for out-of-the-way adventure like a roof rack, and so on.

Is Montero a reliable SUV?

Yes; Edmunds reviewers rank the 2005 model 4.9 out of 5 stars, those on Cars.com rate the 2001 model 4.8 out of 5, and those on RepairPal rate it an average of 4.6. Common problems like crankshaft pulley bolts failing, steering and suspension parts wearing out or getting damaged, and front brakes going bad are easier to try to avoid by staying on top of inspections and getting the SUV serviced regularly at a Mitsubishi dealer.

What are the best Montero model years?

For off-roading, almost any year, although models from between 2003 and 2006 models have 3.8-liter engines, five-speed transmissions, and traction control for higher capability. For use as a family car, 2003 and later models, which have electronic stability control (ESC) for added safety. For comfort preferences, models from between 1999 and 2006, which have more upscale interiors.
