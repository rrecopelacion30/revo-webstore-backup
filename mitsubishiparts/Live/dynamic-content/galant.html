About the Mitsubishi Galant

Compact car of the 1960s or mid-size car of the 2000s and early 2010s. Coupe or sedan, hatchback or station wagon. Base, step-up LS, midrange GS, GSR, or GSX, or high-performance VR-4 and Ralliart. Whatever Galant you drive, it's been one capable, dependable, and above all, affordable ride throughout its storied history

Industry authorities agree, too. Consumer Reports, for example, has called it a competent sedan over the years -- one with a ride quality at once relatively quiet and composed, and with a reasonably agile feel. Edmunds, for another example, lauds the car for generous equipment and a good balance of ride and handling. Car and Driver adds to that appreciation for a roomy interior and clean styling. To be sure, the car's been loaded during its time with features to make it memorably just so -- extendable sun visors, for instance, plus DVD navigation, premium Rockford-Fosgate audio, remote ignition, and more.

FAQs About Mitsubishi Galant

Can I mod my Galant?

Yes. In fact, the car's so cost-effective these days that you can customize for a lot less than if it were new. MSRP for a Galant in 2012, for example, was a rock-bottom $22,000 or so, and Edmunds, for one, puts its value between $1,800 or so and $7,300 nowadays, depending on condition, miles, and options. Kelley Blue Book's KBB.com values it much the same way on average, putting a 2012 ES trim at about $6,800, an FE trim at $5,000 or so, and an SE trim around $6,900. That means plenty of money left over for upgrades

How can I upgrade my Galant for performance?

Say you have a 2012 Galant with a 2.4-liter, in-line, four-cylinder engine. You can get it more power than its standard 160 horsepower and 157 pound-feet of torque by making its burn richer --improving its air-to-fuel ratio. Add a turbocharger for more air for combustion, a cold air intake for better-quality air, and a performance engine air filter for the right balance of flow and filtration. The result: better acceleration, and a more exciting ride.

How can I make the most of the cargo space in my Galant?

Put in cargo accessories like a cargo tray and cargo net in its trunk; they'll better organize your luggage in what's otherwise a tight 13.3 cubic feet. Also, expand that space: install exterior accessories like a roof rack and crossbars, so you can transport gear on the roof

How can I get my Galant a better resale value?

By investing in protection -- replacing your factory floor mats with all-weather floor mats, adding custom seat covers, and installing sunshades, for starters. They'll help shield everything from dirt, muck, and UV heat, which can damage parts, wear out accessories, and drive up repair costs. Keep up with regular maintenance and take care of any repairs the car needs, too, and use OEM parts and accessories for both. A Mitsubishi service center can help.
