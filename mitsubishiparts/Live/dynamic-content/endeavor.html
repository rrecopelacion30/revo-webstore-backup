About the Mitsubishi Endeavor

From its inception in 2004 to the legacy it left in 2011, Endeavor was and still is everything one could hope for in a mid-size SUV. As only Mitsubishi could deliver, it's an eminently safe and mannerly driver, for one -- and yet, one enjoyably sporty, with the performance and versatility spanning the gamut of everyday and off-the-beaten-path. Moreover, it's sophisticated, with an eye to the upscale on its modern interior to match its stunning exterior's good looks. Above all, however, it's got the affordability to match its dependability, making it a fast favorite even today among families, office jet-setters, and lovers of the outdoors alike.

FAQs About Mitsubishi Endeavor Parts and Accessories

Is my Endeavor customizable?

The average price of a used one these days, according to market sources, is between $3,800 and $10,000 or so. This makes the SUV cheap to own, meaning cheaper in the long run to maintain, and so, pretty inexpensive to upgrade. That leaves plenty of prospects. Just be sure to use genuine Mitsubishi parts and accessories for your project -- they'll be top-quality, they'll fit and work better than aftermarket, and they won’t void any factory warranty your ride has left.

Can I upgrade the engine in my Endeavor?

Yes. Add a turbocharger, put in a cold air intake, customize the exhaust -- make it a cat-back system -- and swap out your old catalytic converter for a high-flow type. You'll get better airflow and, because of wider exhaust tubing and a better muffler, higher exhaust flow, which can mean more horsepower and torque.

How can I make my Endeavor look cooler?

Modernize the headlights -- put in LED -- plus, customize the grille, deck it out with a body kit, and upgrade any steel wheels the SUV has: make them aluminum alloy. You can go the extra mile by installing flashy custom wheel accessories like locks and center caps.

How do I protect the value and resale value of my Endeavor?

Other than by staying on top of the maintenance schedule in your owner's manual -- replacing air filters, changing oil filters, and such -- keeping up both its interior and exterior is key. More durable all-weather floor mats, custom seat covers, sunshades, and a car cover can help. The mats and seat covers make better safeguards for plastics and upholstery, and the shades can help keep UV light and heat from frying the electronics. The car cover can help prevent the door panels, windshield, and paint job degrading from exposure to the elements.
