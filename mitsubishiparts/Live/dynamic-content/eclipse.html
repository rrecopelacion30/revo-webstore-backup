About the Mitsubishi Eclipse

Mitsubishi Eclipse is more than a compact car. It's a sports coupe and convertible duo sought after by performance enthusiasts everywhere. From the base GS to the turbocharged GS-T and GSX and the all-wheel-drive GSX, the car's well known for everything from its thrilling acceleratory capability to its cornering stability and dashing good looks. That wasn't just in the 1990s and 2000s, either. By the time the legend became myth in 2012, a Special Edition was offered -- one with a choice of 2.4-liter, four-cylinder or 3.8-liter V6 engine and sporting everything from iconic badging to a contrast-stitched leather interior and, for the coupe version, a sunroof. Eclipse was and still is so beloved, in fact, that it spent four years on the 10Best Cars list at Car and Driver; it remains today an avatar of the Mitsubishi commitment to producing sports cars that stand the test of time.

FAQs About Mitsubishi Eclipse Parts and Accessories

Can I mod my Eclipse?

As the car only went for around $20,000 in its day, give or take (and with depreciation, it's even less expensive nowadays), it's just as cost-effective to own as it is cheap to maintain. In short, it's got tons of prospects.

Can I upgrade the engine in my Eclipse?

The car's first two generations, equipped with 4G63T engines, have plenty of potential for increased horsepower and torque. To get it, consider upgrading the turbocharger, adding a high-flow fuel pump, a cold air intake, and a performance exhaust, and modernizing the radiator and turbo intercooler, just for starters.

What's the best way give my Eclipse a custom look?

Short of a body kit, Adding accent trim, installing a spoiler (or even better, a wing), and installing aluminum alloy wheels with custom wheel accessories like lugs and center caps.

How can I protect the value of my Eclipse?

Keep its interior in top shape, for one. For starters, put in all-weather floor mats, custom seat covers, and sunshades; they'll help safeguard its plastics and upholstery from dirt and spills and its electronics from frying, respectively. Also, consider storing the car under a car cover when you aren't driving it.
