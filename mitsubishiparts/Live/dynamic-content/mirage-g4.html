A Brief History of the Mitsubishi Mirage G4

Mitsubishi Mirage G4 is a sedan that joined the compact segment in 2013. It's known for its good-looking design, comfortable interior, fuel-economical powertrain, and its commitment to safety, via everything from its RISE body construction to its tech features. Today, it remains popular among commuters, day-trippers, and weekend warriors looking for a no-frills city car that's as utilitarian as it is affordable.

FAQs About Mitsubishi Mirage G4 Parts and Accessories

How long can a Mitsubishi Mirage G4 last?

100,000 to 200,000 miles or more. You'll just need to service yours regularly -- rotate the tires every 7,500 miles, flush the cooling system every 30,000 miles, replace the water pump every 60,000 miles, and so on. Check your owner's manual for details on what to do and how often.

Is Mitsubishi discontinuing the Mirage G4?

Yes, likely by 2025 -- and given that the sedan's going for as low as a few thousand dollars used, according to industry expert TrueCar, that makes it a serious prospect for relatively cheap customization. Consider adding all-weather floor mats, seat covers, sunshades to protect your investment, for starters, plus alloy wheels to help it look the part.

How many miles per gallon can a Mitsubishi Mirage G4 get?

The 2024 model, for example, can get 35 mpg city, 41 mpg highway, and 37 mpg combined with a continuously variable (CVT) transmission. You can keep yours as fuel-efficient as possible a couple ways. For one, run light; take any roof rack you've got off when not hauling cargo. Secondly, do regular maintenance -- replace the cabin air filter and engine air filter when your manual says to, for example, plus do oil changes, change the oil filter, and replace the spark plugs at the right time.

How can I improve the performance of my Mitsubishi Mirage G4?

It's as easy as adding a turbocharger (or upgrading any turbo you've got to a supercharger), putting in a cold air intake and a performance air filter, and, for efficiency installing a cat-back exhaust with a high-flow catalytic converter. Don't tune with an aftermarket advance scan tool that you have to plug into the car's onboard diagnostics port; modding that way can void your factory warranty and cause expensive engine problems in the long run. If you need help with any install, contact a Mitsubishi dealer.
