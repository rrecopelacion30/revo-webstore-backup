About the Mitsubishi Precis

Mitsubishi Precis was a subcompact car that debuted in 1987 and ran until 1994. A collaboration between Hyundai and Mitsubishi, it was designed and built with high efficiency and affordability in mind, and offered in both three-door hatchback and four-door sedan body styles. Today, the model's remembered as a budget-conscious and particularly fuel-economical choice for commuters and proverbial weekend warriors everywhere.

Mitsubishi Precis FAQ

How reliable is Mitsubishi Precis?

Fairly; reviewers on Kelley Blue Book's KBB.com, for example, rate the 1992, 1993, and 1994 models 4.5 out of 5 stars. You can keep yours dependable two ways: by staying on top of regular service (namely, by sticking to the schedule in your owner's manual), and by taking care of any problems common to the line sooner rather than later.

What is my Mitsubishi Precis maintenance schedule?

That depends on what your manual says; check it for details. Generally, though, it means doing an oil change, replacing the oil filter, getting a tire rotation, and inspecting for fluid leaks every 7,500 miles, replacing the engine air filter and cabin air filter every 15,000 miles, servicing the brakes (the brake pads and brake rotors, specifically) every 22,500 miles, and so on.

What are some common Mitsubishi Precis problems?

According to RepairPal, you might run into a leaking heater core, faulty manual transmission synchronizers, or a stuck thermostat sometimes. If you need help with repairs, feel free to get in touch with a Mitsubishi dealer to schedule a service appointment.
