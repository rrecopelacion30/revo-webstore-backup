Mitsubishi FAQ

How reliable are Mitsubishi vehicles?

Highly. The auto maintenance experts at RepairPal, for example, give the brand a Reliability Rating of 4.0 out of 5.0, ranking it 6th among 32 brands, with hundreds of vehicles evaluated. Cited are the low ownership costs of its models, the likelihood that they won't end up in the shop as often, and that, when they do, repairs (and the bills for them) aren't too serious. Other industry authorities like Kelley Blue Book's KBB.com, Car and Driver, and J.D. Power add appreciation for affordable pricing, dependability, fuel efficiency, and very competitive warranty coverage -- 10 years/100,000 miles for the brand's powertrains.

What is my Mitsubishi maintenance schedule?

Check your owner's manual for specifics, but here's a few examples of what service to do generally and how often repairs may be needed.

Monthly

  • Wash and wax
  • Clean the interior
  • Inspect and replace any lighting needed
  • Top off fluids for the windshield wipers

Every 3,750 Miles or 3 Months

  • Get a tire rotation and an oil change
  • Replace the oil filter and cabin air filter

Every 7,500 Miles or 6 Months

  • Inspect and replace brake pads and brake rotors
  • Inspect and repair the suspension

Every 15,000 Miles or 12 Months

  • Replace the engine air filter and spark plugs
  • Inspect and service the automatic transmission (if you've got one)
  • Check the brake hoses and driveshaft boots

Put simply, if you want to keep bills down and avoid having to make a service appointment, you should take the best care of your vehicle possible. That means sticking to that service schedule in your manual and, whether you're doing small fixes or big upgrades, use only OEM parts and accessories for the job.
