About the Mitsubishi 3000GT

With DNA from Japan's legendary Mitsubishi GTO, the 3000GT is one speed-demon of a sports car. Even as early as its 1990 release, it was already making a name for itself as a model of craftsmanship belying an enthusiast's expertise. For one, it was known for its powerful performance, owing to packing turbocharged engines like the twin-turbocharged 3.0-liter V6 of VR4 fame, plus all-wheel drive, four-wheel steering, and an active aero package that helped make it an adrenalized thrill-ride, road to track. Secondly, it was a paragon of appointments, with tech and luxury features at the bleeding edge of the heyday of performance cars of 1990s -- everything from leather seats and air-conditioning to a CD player. Furthermore, it was uniquely styled, with a design bordering on iconic -- sometimes retro, occasionally future-facing, always unmistakably Mitsubishi. Above all, however, it was affordable, leaving plenty of cash for customization fresh off the lot.

FAQs About Mitsubishi 3000GT Parts and Accessories

Can I mod my 3000GT?

The average price of one these days, according to the market, is around $23,000. So, the car's not only cheap to own and maintain, it's inexpensive to customize, too. That leaves your prospects wide open. Just be sure to use OEM parts and accessories for the job -- they'll be Mitsubishi quality, they'll fit right and work better than aftermarket, and they won’t void your factory warranty.

What are some popular 3000GT engine upgrades?

Upgrade the turbocharger or twin-turbo, put in a cold air intake, and customize the exhaust -- make it a cat-back system with wider tubing and better muffler. Also, upgrade the catalytic converter to a high-flow type. You'll get better air and exhaust flow, and as a result, more horsepower and torque.

Can I customize my 3000GT exterior?

Yes. Add a body kit, accent trim, and a more modern spoiler or wing, if you want to get it some much-deserved attention. Install aluminum alloy wheels and custom wheel accessories like locks and center caps as the ideal finishing touches.

What are some ways I can protect the value of my 3000GT?

For starters, swap out the factory floor mats for more durable all-weather floor mats, and secondly, add custom seat covers. Together, they'll help safeguard the plastics and upholstery against dirt and spills. Then, put in sunshades to reduce the effects of UV light and heat on the electronics. Also, be sure to store the car under a car cover when you aren't driving it, out of the elements -- it'll go a long way toward keeping the door panels, windshield, and paint job in top shape.
