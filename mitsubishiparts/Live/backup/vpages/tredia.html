A Brief History of the Mitsubishi Tredia

Mitsubishi Tredia was a sedan that made for a reliable, an efficient, and an affordable option among the many subcompact cars for sale between 1982 and 1990. If you're still driving one these days, there's a good chance that it's a vintage find from between 1984 and 1988. Then, the car came in base, L, LS, and Turbo trims with standard front-wheel drive (FWD); four-wheel drive (4WD) was offered for 1984.

How much does a used Mitsubishi Tredia cost these days?

According to the JD Power Values tool, a 1985 model with a turbo engine, for example, or even an L trim from the same year, can go for as little as a few thousand dollars. So, as cheap as you can get one, you should have plenty of cash left over for customization. How much horsepower can Mitsubishi Tredia get?

Anywhere from 88 to 116. To make your ride even more exciting, give it a power bump: add a turbocharger (or trade up to a supercharger), substitute the engine air filter for a performance air filter, mod the exhaust to a cat-back, with a high-flow catalytic converter, and swap out the factory air intake for a cold air intake.

What is my Mitsubishi Tredia maintenance schedule?

You'll find this info in your owner's manual; check it for specifics. Get oil changes, and change the oil filter, replace the air filters and fuel filter, service the transmission, suspension, and brakes, rotate the tires, inspect the cooling system, tune up the engine, and so on, as it calls for.
