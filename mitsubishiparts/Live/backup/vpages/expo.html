A Brief History of the Mitsubishi Expo

Mitsubishi Expo was one seriously versatile minivan, popular almost immediately after its debut in 1992. Compact and maneuverable, practical and fuel economical, stylishly modern, a comfortable ride, and an affordable one, it remains today a great choice for commuters and families on the go.

FAQs About Mitsubishi Expo Parts and Accessories

How much does a used Mitsubishi Expo cost these days?

As little as a few thousand dollars, according to Kelley Blue Book's KBB.com. That means you should have plenty of money left over for upgrades -- alloy wheels, a tuned suspension, more modern tech like infotainment and premium audio systems, cargo accessories like trays and nets to make the most of storage space, you name it.

How reliable is Mitsubishi Expo?

Very. Reviewers on Edmunds, for instance, rate the 1992 model 4.8 out of 5 stars. Reviewers on Cars.com, for another example, rate the 1993 model 4.5 out of 5 stars. KBB reviewers give the 1995 model 4.5, and so on. You can keep yours just as dependable; just be sure to stay on top of regular maintenance -- stick to the schedule in your owner's manual -- and take care of problems common to the line sooner rather than later.

What is my Mitsubishi Expo service schedule?

That depends on what your manual says. Generally, though, you should get an oil change, replace the oil filter, and do a tire rotation every 7,500 miles, change the engine air filter and cabin air filter every 15,000 miles, and so on.

What are some common Mitsubishi Expo problems?

You might run into faulty seat belts, a failed clutch, and a bad torque converter from time to time. If you need help with repairs, feel free to get in touch with a Mitsubishi dealer.
