FIAT® 500 is a city car that released in 2007. You may know it better in the US as a three-door hatchback coupe and two-door cabriolet duo. The model was a throwback to the original FIAT 500, which ran from 1957 to 1975.

Many different models have been available over the years. For instance, a cabrio, FIAT 500C, came out in 2009, followed in 2008 by a sportier variant, FIAT Abarth. FIAT 500e, an all-electric model, then appeared in 2013. Today, the lineup consists of FIAT 500L, a compact minivan that released in 2013, and FIAT 500X, a subcompact crossover SUV that came out in 2020.

Available trims have also varied widely, from Pop, Easy, and Lounge to Sport and Turbo. The same goes for limited editions. The 2011 model year, for example, offered a Pink Ribbon Edition, while the 2014 model year offered both a FIAT 500C GQ Edition and a FIAT 500 1957 Edition. After 2015, there was even a Retro Appearance Package available. You could get it for every FIAT 500 trim except Abarth.

2016 was a notable model year, in which FIAT 500 got a facelift. All models save 500L and 500X were discontinued in 2019.
